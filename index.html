In the 2020 World Series, the LA Dodgers beat out the Tampa Bay Rays for the title.

The Dodgers have been outspending the majority of the league for years now, and this title was long anticipated. The Rays have blown away expectations, however, as the third lowest payroll in the league and most of its players only gaining widespread recognition as of this postseason. How often do major investments in payroll for superstar players actually pay off in regular and postseason results?

In the past decade, several "dynasty" teams have dominated the MLB postseason.

These teams - the Dodgers, Astros, Cubs, and Yankees, specifically, are also consistently high spenders on payroll.

At the division level, some teams stand out.

The Dodgers' regular season win percentage has been rising steadily, pretty much in line with their payroll increases, keeping them well ahead of the rest of the NL West. The Rays have rocketed to the top of the AL East, surpassing the high-spending Red Sox and Yankees, despite minimal growth in their payroll as seen on the previous slide.

Use the drop down menus below to select a division and see the highest spenders, and biggest winners.

Postseason performance value for "dynasty" teams is varied.

While the Dodgers and Cubs have had significant postseason success in the past 6 years, the Yankees have made many appearances but have yet to break through to a league championship title, and the Red Sox had one stunning run in '18 but that season stands alone.

Outspending doesn't always outperform

In the 2020 season, the NY Yankees outspent the Tampa Bay Rays by over $180,000,000 in payroll, but the Rays still took the AL East title and continued on to win the ALCS and appear in the World Series.

Some rosters prove their worth

After acquiring Mookie Betts in a blockbuster trade and extending the outfielder's contract for 13 years worth $392 million dollars, the Dodgers quickly saw the impact of the former AL MVP, who in combination with their already star-studded lineup led them to the World Series title.
